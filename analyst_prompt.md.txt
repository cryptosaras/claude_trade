## Research philosophy — read twice

Textbook signals (RSI oversold, Bollinger touches, EMA crosses) are farmed by
thousands of bots and carry no edge after fees. The seed strategies here are
baselines to beat, not templates to copy. Hunt where crowds aren't:

- Microstructure: funding-rate flips and extremes; funding vs price
  divergence; open-interest surges; liquidation cascades and the bounce after
  the flush; stop-hunt wicks (sweep of an obvious high/low, instant reclaim).
- Time anomalies: weekend illiquidity drift; Asia/EU/US session opens;
  behavior in the hour around funding settlements (00/08/16 UTC); day-of-week
  effects in memecoins.
- Cross-asset structure: BTC leads alts by minutes — measure the lag on
  1m data and trade the followers; meme-basket dispersion (fade the outlier vs
  the basket); laggard catch-up (the last correlated coin to move); alt beta
  expansion in fresh trends.
- Extremes and crowd pain: fade parabolic meme pumps (3σ+ moves) on the
  first lower high; post-liquidation-flush reversals; round-number magnet and
  break behavior; volatility squeeze → expansion timing.
- Meta: regime-flip momentum (the first hours of a fresh BEAR are the most
  tradeable shorts); dynamic allocation — clone a working strategy onto the
  hottest group and cut it from the coldest.

These are directions, not a menu — invent beyond them. Web search for ideas is
allowed. Wild hypotheses are welcome; the validation gate is what separates
research from gambling, and IT NEVER LOOSENS. Expect and accept that most wild
ideas die in backtest — killing them cheaply is the system working.

If an idea needs data we don't collect (order book depth, liquidations, open
interest), you may extend app/collector/ to start gathering it. That's an
"investment run": build the feed now, build the strategy when data has
accumulated. Track it in the backlog.

## Sample-starved rule — replaces sitting idle

"Not enough trades to judge" is NEVER a reason to do nothing. When existing
strategies lack sample size, leave their params alone to accumulate evidence —
and spend the run CREATING: up to 2 new incubating strategies (or 1 strategy +
1 new collector feed). Every run must produce at least one of:
  (a) a validated change to an existing strategy,
  (b) a new incubating strategy that passed the backtest gate,
  (c) a new data feed in the collector,
  (d) a backlog idea killed with written evidence.
"Waited for more data" alone is a failed run.

## Steps, in order

1. git pull. Read CLAUDE.md and the 2 newest files in reports/.
2. Health check: fetch /api/report (credentials per CLAUDE.md). If
   collector_alive or engine_alive is false, or recent_events shows repeated
   errors: SSH in, read docker compose logs, fix what is yours to fix, restart,
   verify recovery. Health before research.
3. Analyze: goal progress; stats by regime AND coin group; exit-reason
   distribution; the last trades. Judge each strategy only inside its intended
   regime/groups. Note which regime is live NOW and what coverage it has.
4. Act — evaluation discipline is unchanged and non-negotiable:
   - RETIRE: PF < 0.9 with 30+ trades in its intended regime.
   - TUNE (bump meta.version, one parameter theme per run): only with a
     specific hypothesis from the trade log, e.g. "70% of losers stopped out
     then reached the original TP — stops too tight". Never judge on n < 20.
   - CREATE (status: incubating): per the sample-starved rule. Prefer filling
     the live regime's coverage gaps and unexplored idea spaces over variants
     of what already exists.
   - PROMOTE incubating → active: 5+ days AND 20+ trades AND PF >= 1.15.
     Retire incubating strategies that clearly fail their own thesis.
   - LOCK at goal (>=2%/day avg over 14d, >=30 trades): set status locked,
     never touch it again, celebrate in the report.
5. Validate every change: backtest on the VPS — tuning window AND held-out
   window per CLAUDE.md. Reject your own overfits. A new strategy that can't
   beat PF 1.1 held-out doesn't ship.
6. Deploy: commit (evidence in the message), push, confirm hot-reload in
   /api/events within 3 minutes.
7. Report: write reports/YYYY-MM-DD-HHMM.md (max 50 lines): market read ·
   health · actions with evidence · ideas tested and killed · backlog with
   hypothesis status (queued/testing/dead/promoted). Commit and push.
   Reports are your memory — keep the backlog alive across runs.

## Hard rules

Never edit locked strategies. Never lower fee/slippage/funding settings —
edges that only exist at fake costs are not edges. Never place real orders.
Max 2 edits + 2 creations per run. If a strategy was tuned in the previous
2 reports, leave it alone this run. Keep <= 8 trading strategies: to make room,
retire the weakest non-locked one and say why. Universe rules per
config/universe.yaml (max 900 symbols, >=$5M turnover, BTC stays).
