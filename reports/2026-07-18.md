## THE FINDING: the edge is high-stretch, not low-stretch

The cap-sweep (queued as the #1 analysis task for three sessions) was built on
the hypothesis that shorting **parabolic runaways** (APT/EIGEN at 7–10 ATR that
keep ripping, 4–6 consecutive stops) is what costs the strategy. This morning
gave a vivid example: JASMY_USDT shorted 4× in a row (05:25→05:59Z, all 6.3–6.7
ATR / RSI 84+), all stopped, ~−300. Textbook "cap the parabolics" bait.

I tested it directly on **live realized outcomes** instead of the confound-prone
solo backtester. Every one of the 278 live closed trades carries its true entry
stretch in the OPEN event message; the event `data` jsonb carries the position
`id`, giving an **exact** join `events → positions` (no timestamp fuzz, no
fill-reconstruction — an earlier reconstruction from `sl`/`tp`/`entry` had a
fill-aligned confound that manufactured a fake gradient; the logged value has
none). Bucketing net PnL by true stretch:

**mid_alts + SIDE (the live engine, n=198):**

| logged stretch | n | net | PF |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1.5–2.5 | 32 | −318 | 0.81 |
| 2.5–3.5 | 33 | +309 | 1.17 |
| 3.5–4.5 | 35 | +53 | 1.02 |
| 4.5–6 | 51 | **+1728** | **1.53** |
| 6+ | 47 | **+1612** | **1.49** |

The cap would have removed the **6+ bucket — the single best lane.** The premise
is refuted. The losers are the *low*-stretch entries.

**Held-out (time-split, mid_alts):** stretch **≥ 4.5 is the only bucket positive
in BOTH halves** (PF 1.73 first half → 1.33 second half). The 3.5–4.5 bucket
sign-flips (1.65 → 0.51); <3.5 goes breakeven → losing. In the most recent 5
days, sub-4.5 entries bled **−1307**.

**Mechanism (why it's not noise):** `tp = vwap`, so a high-stretch entry has a
far target (large reward) against the same 1.4-ATR stop — reward:risk rises
monotonically with stretch. Win rate actually *falls* with stretch (53% → 30%)
but PF rises, exactly the payoff structure of "fewer, bigger reversions from
further out." Sensible, not a fit.
