### What this does and does not tell us (the correction)
My first read was "backtest 1.39/1.52 → pattern alive → edge-decay refuted." That
is wrong, and it is worth spelling out why so the next session does not repeat it:

- **The backtester has no discriminating power here.** Subtract the windows:
  days-7 (n=66, +2303) minus days-2 (n=18, +696) ≈ **48 trades at +1607, PF ~1.4,
  covering Jul 16→21** — a slice that *includes the exact Jul 18→21 days live lost
  money at PF 0.50*. So the backtester prints ~1.4 **both** when live works and
  when live fails. A gauge that reads the same in both states cannot testify about
  the live edge.
- **The evidence is asymmetric.** A *collapsed* backtest (say 0.6) would have been
  strong evidence the pattern died. A non-collapsed one is only weak evidence,
  because for this strategy the backtester has a persistent upward bias and
  effectively can't print low (Jul 21 already saw backtest-style 1.52 vs live
  0.50; [[backtest-vs-live-slot-confound]]). I got the uninformative direction and
  almost read it as exoneration.
- **Nobody claimed the market pattern vanished.** The Jul 21 "edge decay" finding
  was built on *live* forward degradation, uniform across every partition — not on
  a claim that the backtestable signal disappeared. So "refutes the strong form
  (the market changed)" was a strawman. **The Jul 21 live conclusion is untouched.**

Net: this backtest neither confirms nor refutes the live finding. It is not a
court of appeal against live evidence — the confound memo says the backtester is
*less* trustworthy than live, not a way to overturn it.
