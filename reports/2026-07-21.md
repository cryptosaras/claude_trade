## The re-examine: three partitions, three refutations
Deterministic queries over live trades + stored 1h candles (no backtest, so no
pin/twin needed). BTC-trend definitions reused verbatim from `regime.detect`.

### (a) "The forward window was a BTC uptrend, so fading was structurally wrong"
**Refuted, and backwards.** This was the tempting excuse. On the historical
≥4.5 population, BTC-rising is the *best* bucket, not the worst:

| ≥4.5 population | n | net | PF |
|---|---|---|---|
| pre-deploy, BTC rising (above EMA200 & slope>0.001) | 52 | +2683.6 | **1.79** |
| pre-deploy, BTC not rising | 59 | +1172.1 | 1.29 |
| **forward**, BTC rising | 12 | −28.1 | **0.96** |
| **forward**, BTC not rising | 13 | −795.1 | **0.21** |

The strategy historically made most of its money shorting stretch *into* a BTC
uptrend. So the uptrend cannot explain the forward failure — and forward loses
in **both** buckets. A BTC-trend gate is not the fix.

### (b) "The repeat-entry cascades are the loss driver"
**Refuted, and backwards.** The visible signature is TRB ×5, JASMY ×6, SYN ×7,
each stopped then re-entered higher. But historically the repeats are the *good*
half — a cooldown would have removed the edge, not the losses:

| ≥4.5, 2h-gap definition | first entry | repeat (≥1) |
|---|---|---|
| pre-deploy | n=68, PF 1.01 | n=43, PF **2.62** |
| forward | n=9, PF **0.26** | n=16, PF 0.66 |

Stable across 2h/6h/12h gap definitions. This also retires the last version of
the post-stop-cooldown idea (Jul 19 found it cut drawdown, not PF; now we know
it would have cut *profit*).

### (c) "It's one or two bad symbols"
**Refuted.** Forward excluding TRB (−439): still PF **0.69** (n=20). Excluding
TRB *and* JASMY: still PF **0.82** (n=14). Only CRV (+163) and GALA (+123) are
positive; the other 5 symbols all lose.

### What this leaves
Every partition I tested — BTC trend, repeat-vs-first, symbol — shows the
forward sample uniformly worse than its historical counterpart, in **every**
bucket on both sides of every split. That is what edge decay looks like; a
missing filter would concentrate the losses in one bucket, and none does.
Nor is a different floor supported: ≥4.5 is still the best historical bucket
(PF 1.33, n=136, vs 1.14 for all-stretch), and the Jul 20 probe already showed
stretch does not separate forward winners from losers (11.4 vs 12.2 ATR).

**Flip-proximity (declared dead Jul 20) partly resurrects but does not rescue.**
All 6 Jul 20 entries were inside transient SIDE windows, so on *that* day the
filter would have bitten — but it explains only −192 of the −823, and it would
have blocked the single winner (CRV +163) too. It is a symptom of the same
disease as (a): the SIDE label is unreliable during a BTC uptrend. Not a
separate fixable filter; folded into the ADX-lags-structure story.

**Honest caveat:** forward n=25 spans only 2 calendar days and 7 symbols, and
the per-bucket n's are 6–17. "Edge decayed" is the best-supported reading, not a
proven one. That is precisely why I paused rather than retired.
