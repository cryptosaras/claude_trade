### (a) Regime-label lag — **falsified, decisively**
The pre-registered framing was "were losing entries tagged SIDE within N minutes
of a BULL flip?" As suspected, that comes back null trivially — every entry is
hours before the 21:05Z flip. So I tested the stronger claim instead: *was BTC
already trending while the label still said SIDE?*

| entry window | BTC 1h ADX | slope24 | classifier would say |
|---|---|---|---|
| 06:18–06:31 | 13.3 | −0.00248 | SIDE |
| 09:59–13:24 | 10.4–11.3 | −0.00002…+0.00172 | SIDE |
| 14:47–15:02 | 10.9–11.3 | +0.00207…+0.00231 | SIDE |
| 19:07–19:40 | 16.3 | +0.00272 | SIDE |

Re-running `regime.detect`'s logic on the 1h candles at each entry returns **SIDE
for all 19**. BTC's 1h ADX never exceeded **16.3** on Jul 18 — the trend gate is
25. The label was not lagging, flickering, or wrong: BTC genuinely was not
trending when these shorts fired. The BULL flip at 21:05Z was legitimate and
*subsequent*.

Correction to the Jul 19 report: it called mislabeled/late SIDE "the leading
candidate for the forward failure." That is now ruled out. A regime-transition
filter or a "no-entry-within-X-of-a-flip" rule would have changed **nothing** —
it would have blocked zero of the 19 trades.

(NB: the Jul 19 report cited ADX 36.8 as evidence of a strong ramp. That is the
regime snapshot taken *after* the flip, 21:05Z onward — not the state during the
13:24–19:40 cascades. The two are ~8h apart; conflating them is what made the
lag story look plausible.)
