## Tested & REJECTED: upper stretch cap (parabolic filter)
Watchlist item from Jul 14 was a per-symbol cooldown for the APT/EIGEN
repeat-stop pathology. Ctx exposes no per-symbol trade history, so that needs
engine plumbing. Instead I tested the *self-contained* version of the same idea:
an **upper** stretch cap (don't short a coin already N ATR above VWAP — it's
parabolic, not a fade). Pushed a **paused** test variant
(`vwap_revert_short_capped`, env-driven `STRETCH_MAX`) and swept it, 14d:

| cap (ATR) | overall PF | SIDE PF | BEAR PF | n |
|-----------|-----------|---------|---------|---|
| none (v2) | 0.82 | 0.87 | 0.63 | 228 |
| 3.0 | 0.64 | 0.70 | 0.42 | 180 |
| 4.0 | 0.63 | 0.66 | 0.54 | 245 |
| 5.0 | 0.89 | 0.94 | 0.73 | 252 |
| 6.0 | **1.00** | **1.14** | 0.55 | 245 |

- **Tight caps (3–4 ATR) hurt badly.** The mid-stretch (1.5–4 ATR) fades are
  *net winners*; a big-mean-reversion snapback from 4–6 ATR up is a full-VWAP
  TP. The naive "filter the parabolic move" intuition is **wrong** — those
  trades pay for the visible losers.
- **A loose cap (6 ATR) lifts SIDE to 1.14** — an interesting lead: the very
  extreme >6 ATR shorts (APT-at-10-ATR) may be pure losers worth cutting.
- **But this is NOT gate-passing evidence.** Trade counts are non-monotonic
  (180→245→252→245) and can *exceed* the no-cap baseline (228) — removing some
  entries frees slots, so *different* later entries fire. The comparison is
  confounded by position-slot path-dependence; I can't cleanly attribute the
  cap-6 SIDE lift to the filter vs. slot-reshuffle luck. **Not applied to live.**
- Kept the paused variant in the repo as the validation instrument (harmless —
  status `paused`, never trades; `--strategy` only forces it active inside an
  explicit backtest). Precedent: the Jul-11 paused BULL-fade variant.
